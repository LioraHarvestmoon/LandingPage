How does RingConn work?

The RingConn Gen 2 might seem like a simple ring, but it works by collecting data about you through tiny sensors on the inside of the ring.

It has a 3D accelerometer that acts like a highly sensitive motion sensor, tracking your movements throughout the day. This helps it understand your activity levels, whether youâre on a run or just lounging on the couch.

Another sensor is the temperature sensor . It tracks your body temperature, which can be an indicator of overall health and wellness. The RingConn also includes a SpO2 sensor that measures blood oxygen saturation levels. It offers insights into your respiratory health and sleep quality.

Finally, the RingConn uses a PPG sensor, which stands for Photoplethysmography (don't worry, it sounds more complicated than it is!). It means it uses light to measure the heart rate. This helps me track my cardiovascular health and gain insights into how the body responds to exercise or stress.

Long story short, it monitors sleep, stress, heart rate, blood oxygen levels, heart rate variability (HRV), skin temperature, and basic movement.

Is RingConn smart ring accurate?

Measuring the accuracy of any smart ring can be challenging, as, obviously, it often depends on individual factors such as how consistently it's worn and how securely it fits. To gain insights into RingConn's accuracy, I've delved into user reviews and analyzed the available data.

The Gen 2 has introduced several improvements over the original model. However, it's important to note that smart ring technology is still evolving, and no wearable device can match the precision of medical-grade equipment.

Based on my research of user reviews, it seems that while the RingConn is pretty accurate at sleep tracking , it falls short in terms of overall activity monitoring. For activity tracking, experiences vary among users. Some say that step counting can be less precise compared to wrist-worn wearables, which may be inherent to the ring form factor since hand movements don't always correspond to steps.

It appears that the software and algorithms behind RingConn might need an upgrade to keep up with the competition. Nonetheless, RingConn seems to provide reliable data for sleep tracking and other core health metrics.
